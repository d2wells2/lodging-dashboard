How scoring works

      
Each property is scored 0–10 across five dimensions, then combined into a 0–100 composite score using weighted averages.

      

        
📍 Location / AccessProximity to key areas, beach/pool access, ease of transportation, neighborhood quality

        
🎨 Design / ExperienceArchitecture, room quality, aesthetic, unique features, overall wow factor

        
🛎️ ServiceStaff quality, responsiveness, personalization. Excluded for villas/rentals — weight redistributed proportionally.

        
👨‍👩‍👧 Family SuitabilityFit for your traveler profile — age-appropriate amenities, safety, kids' programming

        
⭐ Review ConfidenceConsumer signal quality — volume, recency, positive themes, recurring concerns

      

      

        🎚️
        Use the Weights sliders in the dashboard to re-rank properties for your priorities. Romantic trip? Lower Family, raise Service. Adventure trip? Raise Location.
      

    


    
    

      
Understanding the cost breakdown

      
Every property shows a full cost rollup. The effective nightly rate is the most useful number for comparing properties — it's your real spend after all deductions.

      

        
Nightly rate × nights = Base total

        
+ Local taxes (~15% in Hawaii) = Tax amount

        
+ Resort fee × nights = Resort fees

        
= Gross total

        
− Breakfast credit (FHR / Chase The Edit) Deduction

        
− Property F&B / hotel credit Deduction

        
= Effective total

        
÷ nights = Effective nightly ★

      

      

        💡
        A $1,100/night property with Amex FHR (breakfast $80/day × 4 nights + $100 credit = $420 back) costs $680 effective — cheaper than a "cheaper" $900/night property with no benefits.
      

      
Select your loyalty programs and cards in the intake form. Only programs you hold are applied to the calculation. You can also switch room types on each property card — the cost updates instantly.
